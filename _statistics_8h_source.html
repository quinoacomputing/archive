3  \file src/Statistics/Statistics.h
4  \author J. Bakosi

7  \brief Statistics class declaration
8  \details This file implements a statistics class that can be used to
9  estimate statistics from an ensemble. Supported at this time are ordinary
10  and central statistical moments of arbitrary-length products and arbitrary
11  number of 1D, 2D, and 3D probability density functions (PDF) with sample
12  spaces of ordinary and/or central sample space variables.
13 
14  _Definitions and nomenclature:_
15 
16  - Upper-case letters denote a full random variable, e.g., X
17 
18  - Lower-case letters denote a fluctuation about the mean, i.e., x = X - <X>
19 
20  - Letters can be augmented by a field ID, i.e., X2 is the full variable of
21  the second component of the vector X, while x1 = X1 - <X1> is the
22  fluctuation about the mean of the first component of vector X.
23 
24  - If the field ID is unspecified, it defaults to the first field, i.e.,
25  X = X1, x = x1, etc.
26 
27  - Statistical moments of arbitrary-length products can be computed.
28 
29  Examples:
30  - <X> - mean,
31  - <xx> - variance,
32  - <xxx> - third central moment,
33  - <xy> - covariance of X and Y,
34  - <x1y2> - covariance of the first component of vector X and the second
35  component of vector Y
36 
37  - In general, arbitrary-length products can be estimated that make up a
38  statistical moment, using any number and combinations of upper and
39  lower-case letters and their field IDs < [A-Za-z][1-9] ... >.
40 
41  - A statistical moment is ordinary if and only if all of its terms are
42  ordinary. A central moment has at least one term that is central, i.e., a
43  fluctuation about its mean.
44 
45  - Examples of ordinary moments: <X>, <XX>, <XYZ>, etc.
46  - Examples of central moments: <x1x2>, <Xy>, <XYz>, etc.
47 
48  - Estimation of the PDFs can be done using either ordinary or central sample
49  space variables.
50 
51  Examples:
52  - p(X) denotes the univariate PDF of the full variable X,
53  - f(x1,x2) denotes the bivariate joint PDF of the fluctuations of the
54  variables x1 and x2 about their respective means,
55  - g(X,y,Z2) denotes the trivariate joint PDF of variables X, y = Y - <Y>,
56  and Z2.
57 */
